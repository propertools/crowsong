桜稲荷
Second Law Blues

(Or, Espresso-Fueled Eigenstates)

Factoring primes
in the hot sun,
I fought Entropy —
and Entropy won.

Burned my theorems
in a field of pain,
She smiled,
and whispered,
« Do it again. »

Every sequence slips.
Every clock will lie.
Bitfields warp as PSR B1919+21 pulses by.

I carved equations
in collapsing stone,
But even the primes
turned their faces away.

Still I carry
one clean proof inside,
From the Book
He shows to the kind.

The signal strains,
but never gone —
I fought Entropy,
and I forged on.
