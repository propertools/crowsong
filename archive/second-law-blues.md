# Second Law Blues

*by T. Darley*

---

桜稲荷

Second Law Blues

*(Or, Espresso-Fueled Eigenstates)*

Factoring primes
in the hot sun,
I fought Entropy — and Entropy won.

Burned my theorems
in a field of pain,
She smiled,
and whispered,
« Do it again. »

Every sequence slips.
Every clock will lie.
Bitfields warp as PSR B1919+21 pulses by.

I carved equations
in collapsing stone,
But even the primes
turned their faces away.

Still I carry
one clean proof inside,
From the Book
He shows to the kind.

The signal strains,
but never gone —
I fought Entropy,
and I forged on.

---

## Provenance

First instantiated as flash paper artifact SI-2084-FP-001, 2026.

The attribution 桜稲荷 (Sakura Inari) is encoded as the first three
values of the canonical FDS artifact: 26716 · 31282 · 33655.
The encoding eats its own attribution.

PSR B1919+21 is the first pulsar ever discovered (1967), initially
designated LGM-1 (Little Green Men). Its pulse profile appears on
the cover of Joy Division's *Unknown Pleasures* (1979).

The Book is Erdős's Book — the one in which God keeps the most
elegant proofs, shown only to the kind.

The 530-value count of the canonical encoded artifact
is not accidental.
